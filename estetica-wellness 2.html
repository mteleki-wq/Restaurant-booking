Who I Am

My work has always lived at the intersection of people, presence, and purpose.

I began my career as a hair and makeup artist, working closely with clients during moments of change — transitions, reinvention, weddings and milestones. Clients often confided things they hadn't shared with anyone else. I was always careful with the guidance I offered, yet time and again I was met with gratitude — not for what I did, but for how I made them feel: seen, safe, and understood.

These interactions sparked a deep interest in human mind and behaviour. I became curious about the choices people make — their careers, lifestyles, communication patterns, and health. This led me into vocational education, then mentoring and leadership, while continuing as a freelance artist to remain connected to industry practice.

In the midst of profound grief and emotional trauma, a calling emerged — not as a choice, but as a necessity. A decade-long pilgrimage, inward. I devoted myself to healing through deep personal work, study and embodied practice. It was through this journey that I found breathwork, nervous system regulation and transformational therapy.

Today I help people feel safe enough to trust themselves again. Through embodied, nervous system informed work, I guide my clients toward real transformation — not by pushing changes, but restoring the inner conditions where change becomes possible.
