Assessment Requirement 1

Prepare a Draft & Final Budget for the Next Two Financial Quarters

In consultation with the accounts department — demonstrating consultation, factor analysis, and draft/final versions within designated timelines.

Part 1 of Student Guide Sections A → J 7 steps

Steps to complete →

Section A
Set Your Business Context
▼

What the assessment requires

Describe the business you are budgeting for — its type, what the budget covers (sales, food, wages, overheads), and the budget period (Q1 and Q2 dates).

Instructions Tab
  • Open the Instructions tab in your Excel workbook
  • Use your restaurant name, cuisine type and budget period from that tab
  • Write 3–5 sentences: what is the business, what does the budget cover, and what are the Q1 and Q2 dates
  • Fill in the YOUR RESPONSE box in Section A of the Student Guide
⚠ Do not use the trainer’s example restaurant. Your response must describe YOUR restaurant from the Instructions tab.
Section B
Consultation Process
▼

What the assessment requires

Consult relevant parties before preparing your budget. Name at least 3 people or roles you consulted and explain what you discussed with each one.

Assumptions TabBudget_Q1_Q2 Tab
  • Name at least 3 people: e.g. Accounts Officer, Head Chef, Restaurant Supervisor
  • For each person, write 2–3 sentences: what you asked them and what they told you
  • Connect each consultation to a specific figure in your Assumptions tab
  • Fill in Section B of the Student Guide
⚠ Generic responses like “I consulted staff” will not meet the standard. Name who, what they said, and where you applied their advice.
Section C
Data Identified, Accessed & Interpreted
▼

What the assessment requires

Identify what data you used, where you got it from, and what it told you. Evidence that you analysed real information before entering numbers.

Assumptions Tab
  • List data sources: previous sales records, supplier price lists, payroll records, utility bills, booking data, food cost reports
  • For each source, explain how it informed a specific figure in your Assumptions tab
  • Write 4–6 sentences describing what the data revealed and how it changed your estimates
Section D
Internal & External Factors
▼

What the assessment requires

Identify the factors with the most potential to influence income and expenditure. Each factor must connect to a specific workbook decision.

Assumptions TabBudget_Q1_Q2 Tab
  • Internal: Labour costs (Labour Planner), organisational objectives (Budget tab rows 30–34), planned promotions (Marketing line)
  • External: Commodity prices (food cost % in Assumptions cell C23), economic conditions, market trends
  • For each factor, write the specific figure from your workbook that reflects it
Section E
3 Authorities Consulted
▼

What the assessment requires

Consult at least 3 different authorities, inform them of decisions you are taking, and solicit their advice. Document each authority’s specific advice and where you applied it.

Assumptions TabBudget_Q1_Q2 Tab
  • Authority 1 (e.g. Accounts Officer) — what they advised + which cell/tab you updated
  • Authority 2 (e.g. Head Chef) — what they advised + which cell/tab you updated
  • Authority 3 (e.g. Supervisor) — what they advised + which cell/tab you updated
  • Fill in the 3-row table in Section E of the Student Guide
✔ This section also feeds into Part 2 — your Decision Report. Note the advice now as you’ll need it again.
Section F
Task Allocation & Deadlines
▼

What the assessment requires

Set yourself and colleagues deadlines for specific tasks. Show a structured, consultative process with named responsibilities.

  • Create a 7-day timeline (or adapt to your scenario)
  • Each day: name the task, who is responsible, and what was produced
  • Include: data gathering, consultant meetings, draft completion, feedback, modifications, final budget
Sections G · H · I · J
Draft Budget → Decisions → Modifications → Final
▼

What the assessment requires

Present your two-quarter budget, explain every major decision, document modifications after consultation, and present the final budget to relevant parties.

Budget_Q1_Q2 Tab — all figuresAssumptions Tab — food cost %, labour %
  • G: Pull Q1 and Q2 figures from Budget_Q1_Q2 tab (cells C8, D8, C11, C16, C26, C27). Do NOT use trainer example figures.
  • H: For each budget line: the figure, why you chose it, and what data or advice informed it
  • I: Document at least 2 modifications requested after the draft was circulated
  • J: Name who received the final budget and what you communicated to each person
⚠ All figures must come from YOUR workbook. Do not use $180,000 / $195,000 or any trainer example numbers.
Assessment Requirement 2

Produce a Decision Report Alongside Your Budget

While producing your budget: consult 3 authorities, analyse internal & external factors, set deadlines, and explain each decision in a brief report.

Part 2 of Student Guide Decision Report 3 steps

Steps to complete →

Before Writing
Gather Your Decision Evidence
▼

What you need before you write

The decision report references specific figures and advice. Make sure you have these ready before you start writing.

Assumptions TabBudget_Q1_Q2 Tab
  • Note your Q1 and Q2 total sales figures (Budget_Q1_Q2, cells C8 and D8)
  • Note your food cost % (Assumptions tab, cell C23) and why you chose it
  • Note your labour total and the award rates used (Assumptions tab, Labour Planner rows 29–37)
  • Note your contingency amount and the risks it covers (Budget tab, row 25)
  • Note your organisational objectives (Budget tab, rows 30–34)
Part 2 — Main Task
Write the Decision Report
▼

What the assessment requires

A brief report explaining in first person why you made each major budget decision. Every claim must reference a specific figure or tab. NOT a list of steps.

  • Write 3–5 paragraphs. Each paragraph = one decision
  • Para 1: Revenue estimate — why Q1 is conservative, why Q2 is higher (reference C8 and D8)
  • Para 2: Food cost % — benchmark, why you chose it, which authority advised it (reference C23)
  • Para 3: Labour — roles, hours, award rates, total cost (reference Labour Planner)
  • Para 4: Contingency — amount, risks it covers (reference row 25 of Budget tab)
  • Para 5: Organisational objectives — name each and link to a budget decision
📌 Write in first person throughout: “I decided...”, “I chose...”. Never write in third person.
⚠ This is the most commonly lost marks section. Generic statements without specific figures will not meet the standard.
Quality Check
Review Your Decision Report
▼

Before moving to Part 3 — check every paragraph

Each paragraph must contain all three elements to meet the assessment standard.

  • ✓ A specific figure from your workbook (cell reference or amount)
  • ✓ A reason for that decision (data, authority advice, or business objective)
  • ✓ Written in first person (“I decided...” / “I chose...”)
📌 If any paragraph has a figure but not the reason — rewrite it before submitting.
Assessment Requirement 3

Hold 3 Review Meetings During the Budget Cycle

Review actual performance against estimated, investigate significant deviations, produce a brief budget report, and store and file all reports according to policy.

Part 3 of Student Guide Meetings 1 · 2 · 3 4 steps

Steps to complete →

Before All Meetings
Enter Actuals in Your Excel Workbook
▼

Do this first — before writing any meeting reports

All figures in Part 3 must come from your Monthly_Review tab. Enter actual figures first so variance and Fav/Unfav columns calculate automatically.

Monthly_Review Tab
  • Open Monthly_Review tab — find the yellow Actual cells (columns E, I, M)
  • Enter Month 1 actuals in row 5: Sales Actual (E5), Food Actual (I5), Wages Actual (M5)
  • Enter Month 2 actuals in row 6: E6, I6, M6
  • Enter Month 3 actuals in row 7: E7, I7, M7
  • Check Variance and Fav/Unfav columns have calculated automatically
  • Check the Quarter Totals in rows 14–15
📌 Budget columns (D, H, L) auto-fill from Budget_Q1_Q2 ÷ 3. You only enter the ACTUAL amounts in yellow cells.
Meeting 1 — End of Month 1
Monthly Review & Budget Report
▼

What the assessment requires

Review Month 1 actual performance against the budget. Identify variances, explain findings, take corrective action, write a brief budget report, and file it.

Monthly_Review Row 5Review_Reports Row 5
  • Variance table: Copy figures from row 5 of Monthly_Review into the Meeting 1 table in the Student Guide
  • Findings: For each line — was it FAV or UNFAV? By how much? What caused it?
  • Actions: List 3–5 specific corrective actions that address the variances
  • Budget report: 3–5 sentences in formal management style — overall result, main reason, action taken
  • Filing: Document where the report was saved (e.g. Finance Folder / Q1 / Month 1)
Meeting 2 — End of Month 2
Performance Review & Budget Report
▼

What the assessment requires

Review Month 2 performance and assess whether Month 1 corrective actions worked. Identify improvements and any remaining concerns.

Monthly_Review Row 6Review_Reports Row 7
  • Copy Month 2 figures from row 6 of Monthly_Review into the Meeting 2 variance table
  • Compare to Month 1 — did performance improve, worsen, or stay the same?
  • Explain what drove the change with specific figures
  • Continue or adjust the Month 1 corrective actions based on the results
  • Write your Month 2 budget report and document where it was filed
📌 The assessor expects to see that your Month 1 actions had a measurable effect by Month 2. Connect actions to results explicitly.
Meeting 3 — End of Quarter 1
Quarter Review, Deviations & Q2 Planning
▼

What the assessment requires

The most important meeting. Review the full quarter, investigate any variance over 5%, take corrective action for Q2, write the Quarter 1 budget report, and file everything.

Monthly_Review Row 14Review_Reports Rows 9 & 14–17
  • Quarter totals: Use row 14 of Monthly_Review (Q1 total row) for the variance table
  • Significant deviations: Any line over 5% must be formally investigated. Formula: (Actual − Budget) ÷ Budget × 100
  • Investigation: List internal causes (staffing, wastage, menu pricing) and external causes (supplier prices, weather, demand)
  • Q2 actions: List 4–6 specific corrective actions for next quarter
  • Quarter report: 4–6 sentences covering overall result, main deviation, cause, and Q2 plan
  • Filing: Finance Folder / Q1 / Quarter Review — document all files stored
⚠ If no line exceeded 5% variance, state that clearly. The assessment still needs the investigation section completed.
